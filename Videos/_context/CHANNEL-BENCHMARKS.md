# What the successful channels do, and what KiqIQ takes from it

Reviewed: Tifo Football (The Athletic), Pythagoras in Boots, James Lawrence Allcott, Statman Dave, Tactical Football Analysis, The Coaches' Voice. Below is what they share and how it maps to this channel.

## What works for them

- One clear idea per video. Tifo lets the story lead, avoids rumors and the news cycle, and stays substantive rather than reactive. Take: every KiqIQ video is one counterintuitive thesis, never a news react.
- Animation is the visual engine. Tifo's signature is animation and motion graphics, not match footage. Take: our motion typography and chart reveals are the equivalent. The footage-free model is proven by the biggest channel in the lane.
- Depth and original research carry the brand. Pythagoras in Boots sells "elite analysis" on tactical fit and mechanism, not hot takes. Take: the verified fact sheet and the mechanism-to-consequence read are the product.
- A strong hook decides the video. Retention research: a hook inside the first 15 seconds keeps about 65 percent of viewers to the three-minute mark; without one, retention falls below 45 percent. Take: the first-10-seconds hook rule is non-negotiable.
- Consistency over volume. Tifo runs a weekly cadence and a companion podcast. Take: weekly long-form, sustained, beats sporadic bursts.
- Clean, legible thumbnails. One subject, few words, high contrast. Take: already built into thumb.py.

## Length

Derive the target per video from the strong performers on that specific player during the competitive scan. Default working range for a single-thesis essay is 8 to 12 minutes. Go longer only when the research genuinely fills it.

## Repurposing to Shorts

The strong channels feed discovery with short clips. Cut 15 to 35 second Shorts from each long-form video (one stat, one reveal, one hook), 3 to 7 per week. Each Short points back to the full essay.

## What to avoid (their failure modes and the genre's)

- Chasing rumors and the news cycle. It ages badly and invites correction.
- Talking-head sameness with no original data. Easy to make, hard to stand out, and exposed under the 2026 inauthentic-content policy.
- Copying a competitor's structure. Leads only, never the skeleton.

## The KiqIQ gap

Most of these are animation-led explainers or talking-head tactics. Few run a strictly footage-free, single-thesis, verified-data format with an on-screen source line for every figure in a stylized art skin that changes with the angle. That accuracy-first lane is the differentiator and the monetization shield.

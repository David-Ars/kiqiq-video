# AI safeguards checklist (pre-upload)

Protects monetization under YouTube's 2026 inauthentic-content policy and the AI-disclosure rules. AI narration and AI visuals are allowed. What gets demonetised is templated, mass-produced content with no original research or editorial contribution. This channel's defence is verified data and an original thesis, made visible.

Run this before every upload.

## Originality and research
- [ ] The video is built on our own verified research and an original thesis, logged on the fact sheet.
- [ ] Wording and structure are original, not modelled on a competitor's video.
- [ ] Every figure is logged with its source and tier on the fact sheet (the source record; not shown on screen).
- [ ] The analysis is ours, not a read-out of public facts.

## Visuals
- [ ] Player imagery is rendered in the art style with the face kept abstract and non-literal. Team imagery uses no single face and no real crest or sponsor logo.
- [ ] Plain kit, era colours, no club logos or sponsor marks.
- [ ] The image prompt did not name the player. Identity comes from build, hair, kit, posture and context, not a literal face.
- [ ] Gemini/nano-banana visible watermark inpainted out seamlessly (content-aware fill, not a crop), checked at 100% zoom: not noticeable, no smudge or repeated-texture artefact. Note: SynthID stays embedded, so the image is still detectable as AI and the YouTube disclosure still applies.

## Disclosure
- [ ] If any image could read as realistic footage of a real person or event, tick YouTube's "altered or synthetic content" box at upload. Stylized art usually does not require it; disclose when in doubt. Disclosure does not reduce reach or earnings.

## Format variation (anti-mass-produced signal)
- [ ] Intro and structure vary from recent episodes; no copy-paste template feel.
- [ ] Narration is original and information-dense, not filler around a thin stat.

## Cadence sanity
- [ ] This week's research is solid. If it is not, the publish date slips, not the standard. Accuracy is the channel.
